## What Is a Maiden Name?

A maiden name is traditionally a woman's surname before marriage. However, its significance extends far beyond marital status. Your maiden name — or more broadly, your birth surname — is the name that first carried your energetic signature into the world. ### The Difference Between a Maiden Name and a Married Name

### How Elements Manifest in Surnames

Surnames often originated from occupations, locations, physical characteristics, or ancestral traits. Each of these origins resonates with a particular elemental frequency:

**Water Surnames** often derive from rivers, seas, or occupations near water. Names like *Brooks*, *Shepherd* (one who guides across waters), *Fisher*, or *Moore* (moorland, often near water). Those with Water-element maiden names possess deep intuition, emotional wisdom, and a natural ability to flow through life's changes.

**Fire Surnames** arise from occupations involving fire or forge: *Smith*, *Black*, *Cole* (coal), *Chandler* (candle maker). If your maiden name carries Fire energy, you embody transformation, passion, and the power to illuminate darkness.

**Earth Surnames** connect to the land: *Stone*, *Wood*, *Fields*, *Hill*, *Clay*. Your stability, patience, and nurturing nature are gifts from the Earth element embedded in your ancestral name.

**Metal Surnames** derive from refined materials or sharp objects: *Silver*, *Gold*, *Cutler* (knife maker), *Armstrong*. Metal names carry precision, clarity, and strength of spirit.

**Wood Surnames** connect to forestry and growth: *Forrest*, *Green*, *Bush*, *Oakley*. Wood names speak of expansion, vision, and the upward-reaching energy of life itself.
