## The Origin of the Name Aurora

### Aurora — Roman Goddess of the Dawn

In Roman mythology, **Aurora** was the goddess of the dawn — counterpart to the Greek **Eos** — who rose each morning from the ocean in her saffron-colored robes to open the gates of heaven for the sun to ride through. Ancient poets described her as rosy-fingered, as golden, as the one who scatters the darkness before her.

Her task was not to be the sun. It was to *precede* the sun — to prepare the way, to announce the arrival of what was coming, to make the transition from night to day possible. This is the Aurora archetype in its purest form: **the herald of what comes after.**

The Roman poet Ovid described her relationship with the sun as one of perpetual, loving service — she rises before him every morning, not from obligation, but from love. There is something in this that speaks to the Aurora character: they show up before others do, they prepare what needs to be prepared, and they do it with a quality of radiance that makes the arrival of whatever follows more beautiful.

### The Aurora Borealis

The name was also given, by analogy, to the **aurora borealis** — the northern lights — because of the similarity between the colors of dawn and the colors of that extraordinary atmospheric phenomenon. The aurora borealis (and its southern counterpart, aurora australis) are among the most breathtaking natural spectacles on Earth: curtains of green, gold, violet, and crimson light that dance in the polar skies.

The connection between the name Aurora and the northern lights has deepened the name's association with **natural wonder, rare beauty, and the kind of luminous presence that stops people in their tracks.**

### Aurora Through Time

The name has been used consistently since Roman antiquity, though it experienced particular revival in the Romantic period, when classical names were fashionable. **Sleeping Beauty** — in its oldest French form, Charles Perrault's *La Belle au Bois Dormant* — gave the name to the princess who would be awakened by a prince's kiss: Aurora. More recently, **Disney's Aurora** has introduced the name to new generations, though the name's depth far exceeds any single cultural reference.
