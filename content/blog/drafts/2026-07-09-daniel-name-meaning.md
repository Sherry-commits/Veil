## What Does the Name Daniel Mean?

The name **Daniel** is one of the most globally beloved names in human history — and its meaning reveals why. From the Hebrew **דָּנִיֵּאל** (*Daniyel*) — *Dan* (to judge) + *El* (God) — Daniel means **"God is my judge"** or **"God is the judge."**

This is a declaration of extraordinary liberation. To say "God is my judge" is to say: *no human tribunal, no social pressure, no earthly authority holds the final word over my soul.* It is the foundation of the Daniel character: the capacity to stand in the presence of the most powerful human forces imaginable and remain, quietly, completely, unmoved.

## The Origin of the Name Daniel

### The Biblical Daniel

The Book of Daniel tells the story of a young Jewish exile brought to the court of Babylon — one of the most powerful empires in the ancient world. Daniel is recognized for his unusual wisdom and rises to a position of influence, but never loses his essential allegiance. When commanded to stop praying, he prays by his open window as he has always done. When thrown into the lions' den for this defiance, he emerges unharmed.

What the Daniel narrative captures is the archetype of **integrity under pressure** — the soul whose moral center is so deep and so stable that external force cannot reach it. The lions are real. The den is real. The threat is real. And Daniel simply stands there, because he has nothing to prove to any power that does not hold the final verdict.

This is the Daniel essence: **the capacity to be present to danger without being governed by it.**

### Daniel Through History

The name Daniel spread through Jewish communities and then into Christian and Islamic tradition (where the prophet is recognized as **Danyal**), making it one of the most cross-cultural names in human history. In medieval Europe, it was carried by saints and scholars. In the modern world, it consistently ranks among the top 10 names in the United States, UK, Ireland, Australia, and across Latin America.
